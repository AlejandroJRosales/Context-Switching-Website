Artificial and Biological Neural Networks: The Similarities

Biological Neural Networks

We will start with talking about what biological nueral networks are. Such as how they operate and what are their functions. The reason we begin with human neural networks is because it will be more easy to understand since we all can relate to their functions, rather than starting with a more foregin conecpt such as artificial neural networks.

Biological neural networks are a collection of brain cells connected together at a point, that when activated excite other cells in their collection. Brain cells, or neurons, like any other cell, are intricate however, for our talk today we only need to know three parts of the neuron: cell body, axon terminals or synapses, and dendrites.
