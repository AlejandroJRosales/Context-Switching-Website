What is Heritability

Formal

I will list some more formal definitions given by other reputable sites to see if one catches, then I will give a more laymen explanation:

  • Heritability, as described in "The Paradox of Intelligence: Heritability and Malleability Coexist in Hidden Gene-Environment Interplay" by Bruno Sauce and Louis D. Matzel is, "a statistic that, as commonly interpreted, captures how much of the variation on a trait is due to genetic differences"
  • "Heritability is a measure of how well differences in peopleâ€™s genes account for differences in their traits" as described by MedlinePlus, a government website.
  • A more analystical definition given by Nature.com is that heritability is, "the proportion of phenotypic variation (\(V_{P}\)) that is due to variation in genetic values" (\(V_{G}\)).

Laymen

Ok, so those are more formal definitions. Let us try to describe, from those definitions, what heritability is in laymen's terms. Heritability tells someone how much variation of trait x, is due to genetic differences from the mean. Now, the statistic describing heritability lies between 0.0 and 1.0, or [0, 1]. A heritability statistics of say 0.0 means that the trait has no, 0, genetic component. Whereas a heritability or 1.0, means that the trait is completely heritable.
