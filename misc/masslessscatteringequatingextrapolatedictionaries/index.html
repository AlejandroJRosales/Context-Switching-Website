Geometry of Conformal Inversions

Minkowski Space and Metric

Minkowski space is denoted as \(\mathbb{M}=\mathbb{R}^{1,3}\) with the Cartesian coordinates \(X^\mu, \mu=0,1,2,3\). The Minkowski metric is defined \(\eta_{\mu \nu}=\operatorname{diag}(-1,1,1,1)\) and has the signature \((1,3)\). The light cone of the origin to null infinity is mapped using the conformal inversion mapy \(\mathbb{M}\). The authors mention embeded space formalism is eaiser to study conformal transormations with conformal compactification of \(\mathbb{M}\).

Poincaré Patch on Einstein Cylinder

Let \(Z^{I}\) with index \(I=-1,0,...,4\) be coordinates on embedding space \(\mathbb{R}^{2,4}\) where the light cone of origin \(\mathbb{R}^{2,4}\) is cut out by:

\(\left(Z^{-1}\right)^2+\left(Z^0\right)^2\)

\(=\left(Z^1\right)^2+\left(Z^2\right)^2+\left(Z^3\right)^2+\left(Z^4\right)^2\)

where the origin \(Z^{I}=0\) is removed. The remaining light code is positively rescaled with quotient \(Z^I \sim t Z^I, t \in \mathbb{R}_{+}\), arriving at Penrose's conformal compactification of \(\mathbb{M}\).

Celestial Quadric

Given now that the light point no longer contains an origin, it no longer contains points at \(Z^{-1}=Z^{0}=0\), meaning for the quoitent we can chose \(Z^{I}\) to satisfy:

\(\left(Z^{-1}\right)^2+\left(Z^0\right)^2\)

\(=\left(Z^1\right)^2+\left(Z^2\right)^2+\left(Z^3\right)^2+\left(Z^4\right)^2\)

\(=1\)

To do so, the authors mention performing the positive rescaling \(Z^{I}\rightarrow Z^{I}/\sqrt{(Z^{-1})^{2}+(Z^{0})^{2}}\), where the resulting space is topoglogically \(S^{1}\times S^{3}\).

This resulting topological space is a celestial quadric, such that the complement of the hyperplane in the space \(Z^{-1}+Z^{4}=0\) consists of two Poincaré Patches that are each a copy of \(\mathbb{M}\). The embeddings for the positive rescalings are defined as:

\(X^\mu \mapsto Z^I= \pm\left(\frac{1+|X|^2}{2}, X^\mu, \frac{1-|X|^2}{2}\right)\)

where \(|X|^2=\eta_{\mu \nu} X^\mu X^\nu\) is the Lorentzian norm.
