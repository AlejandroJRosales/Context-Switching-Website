Simple Graph Definition

Visual

First, lets discuss what is meant by a graph. A visual we should have in mind. The reason being because often when one hears, "graphs" what comes to mind are bar graphs, pie charts, histograms, etc. However, when refering to graphs in much of computer science and math what computer scienctists and mathematicians really are refering to are a set of nodes often connected together by edges. Like shown below.

The lines are called edges, while the circles are called nodes. Often times nodes are shown as circles but not always.

What this all means is that when thinking of graphs for math or computer science (CS) one should have the image above in mind.

Mathematical

A graph is an unordered pair \(G = (V, E)\) comprising of a set of vertices \(V\) (sometimes called nodes or points) and a set of edges (called links or lines) defined as

$$E \subseteq \left\{\left\{x, y\right\}\, |\, x, y \in V\, and\, x \neq y\right\}$$
