Inner Product Estimation

The rows \(A_p^{\ell}\) and columns \(F_q^{\ell}\) first must be accessed from QRAM to preform the mapping:

\(\frac{1}{K} \sum_{p, q}|p\rangle|q\rangle \mapsto \frac{1}{K} \sum_{p, q}|p\rangle|q\rangle\left|\bar{P}_{p q}\right\rangle\left|g_{p q}\right\rangle\)

where \(\bar{P}_{p q}\) is the estimated inner product estimation of input rows and \(A_p^{\ell}\) and kernel columns \(F_q^{\ell}\). The true value of the inner product is calculated as \(P_{p q}=\frac{1+\left\langle A_p^{\ell} \mid F_q^{\ell}\right\rangle}{2}\), such that the inner product estimation differs by a choosen constant \(\epsilon\). Here, the normilization factor is \(K=\sqrt{H^{\ell+1} W^{\ell+1} D^{\ell+1}}\) and \(|q_{pq}\rangle\) is some garbage state.
